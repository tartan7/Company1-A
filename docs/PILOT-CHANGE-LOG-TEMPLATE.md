*Date: YYYY-MM-DD / Version: 1.0*
*Owner: CMO / Account Lead*
*Pilot: [Client Name / Pilot ID]*

## 1. Purpose
Use this template to maintain a clear, auditable record of pilot changes over time.
Log each material change within 1 business day, including rationale, impact, and follow-up ownership.

---

## 2. What to Log

Record any change that affects one or more of the following:

- Scope, deliverables, or acceptance criteria
- Timeline, milestones, or sequence of work
- KPI definitions, targets, or measurement method
- Budget, resource allocation, or staffing
- Technical architecture, integrations, or data dependencies
- Governance, compliance, or risk posture

Do not log routine status updates that do not alter delivery direction.

## 4. Status Definitions

- **Proposed:** Change identified and documented; pending decision.
- **Approved:** Stakeholders accepted change; execution scheduled.
- **Implemented:** Change completed and reflected in current operations/docs.
- **Reverted:** Previously implemented change rolled back.

When a change is set to `Reverted`, include reason and reference the replacement or rollback action.

---

## 5. Change Quality Checklist

Before marking a change as `Approved` or `Implemented`, confirm:

1. The change has a clear business or delivery trigger.
2. Impact across `scope/timeline/budget/risk` is explicitly captured.
3. One accountable owner is assigned.
4. Notification recipients are listed.
5. Related artifacts (decision log, risk register, remediation plan, stakeholder update) are linked.

---

## 6. Weekly Governance Review

1. Confirm all material changes from the week are logged.
2. Validate statuses (`Proposed`, `Approved`, `Implemented`, `Reverted`) are current.
3. Check that implemented changes are reflected in baseline docs and trackers.
4. Escalate high-impact proposed changes lacking owner or decision date.
5. Include top 3 change entries in the weekly stakeholder update.
